## Question

> When premises and conclusion share structure, but still
> do not justify each other — what content-level
> contradiction remains?

v4.3, v4.5, and v4.7 cumulatively retired 134 of 143
v4.0 false-supports via three contamination-safe runtime
patches: marker extension, structural cycle guard, and
modality consistency check. The remaining 9 cases all sit
in the v4.6 MISSING_BRIDGE_RULE cluster. They carry no
surface marker, no structural cycle, and no modal shift —
v4.6 already showed no safe probe in its warrant bank
could rescue them. v4.8 asks whether *content-level*
probes can.

## Method

* **Case selection:** the 9 chains in the v4.0 corpus that
  still audit as `LOGICALLY_SUPPORTED + CAUSAL_CHAIN` under
  the v4.7-patched auditor. All are MISSING_BRIDGE_RULE
  per the v4.6 taxonomy.
* **Replay:** extended replay records per-premise and
  per-conclusion content tokens, the overlap set, the
  support path, and the originating v4.1 strategies.
* **Classification:** a deterministic priority cascade
  assigns each case *exactly one* `ContentFailure` value
  (9 closed values).
* **Counterfactual probes (C1–C5):** five simulations
  of alternative content checks. Each is a predicate
  evaluated on every residue case and on every
  protected VALID chain.
* **Negative controls:** 60 hand-authored content fixtures
  — twenty contradiction pairs, twenty inversion cases,
  twenty valid content-preserving chains.

## Recommendation

**`CONTENT_FAILURE_LOCALIZED`** — the 9 residual
false-supports are not diffuse. They concentrate into two
named families, each tied to one domain. **Both clusters
admit a safe content-level probe** with zero contamination:
C1 covers DIRECT_CONTRADICTION (4 cases), C2 covers
PROPERTY_REVERSAL (5 cases). A future runtime patch
deploying these two probes would, in principle, retire the
entire v4.0 false-support residue.

## Reflection

The directive asks: *"When does semantic overlap stop being
evidence — and become contradiction in disguise?"*

Using only the v4.8 class counts, rescue rates,
contamination, and probe agreement:

* **Two named clusters** absorb all nine cases; the residue
  is fully named (entropy = 0.991 bits, two near-equal
  families).
* **Zero UNKNOWN** — the closed content taxonomy covers the
  post-marker, post-structural, post-modality residue.
* **Two safe probes** rescue all nine between them
  (C1 covers four, C2 covers five, intersection empty —
  cluster-disjoint coverage).
* **Zero protected VALID chains** trigger either safe
  probe. The contradiction-pair and polarity-flip patterns
  are sharp enough to distinguish the residue from
  benchmark prose.
* **C5 entity_consistency_check** catches everything
  (all nine cases) at the cost of five hundred
  twenty-eight contaminations — the blast-radius evidence
  that breadth is not the same as discipline.

Semantic overlap stops being evidence and becomes
contradiction in disguise precisely when the conclusion
lexically **reverses** a polarity stated in a premise
(C2's territory) or **negates** a procedural / temporal
condition stated in a premise (C1's territory). Both
patterns share *content tokens* with the premise (which is
why earlier modal / structural / marker probes missed
them) but invert the truth-conditional content. The
discipline of v4.8 is to identify those two lexical
inversion families precisely — using narrow contradiction-
pair tables, not domain vocabulary — and to confirm that
both tables are contamination-free.
