# v5.3 — Corpus Construction Bias Audit

**Status:** read-only self-audit. No runtime patches, no
taxonomy renaming, no probe modifications, no rewrites of
v5.0/v5.1/v5.2 artifacts.
**Branch:** `feature/corpus-construction-bias-audit`.
**Artifacts:** `artifacts/v5_3/report.json`,
`artifacts/v5_3/rewrite_diff.json`.
**Tests:** 50 new under `tests/corpus_bias_audit/`; full
regression green.

## Question

> Would the v5.2 taxonomy still generalize if we removed
> all human corpus shaping?

v5.2 reported `TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES` on a corpus that I
hand-authored and then rewrote — twice — to align with
the v5.0 probes' strict vocabulary. v5.3 reconstructs the
pre-rewrite (RAW) version of every v5.2 chain, replays
the v5.2 classifier and probes on the RAW corpus, and
measures how much of the reported generalization came
from the rewrites.

## Method

* **Frozen inputs (Aufgabe 3)** — `artifacts/v5_0/
  taxonomy.json` (8 ``MT_*`` classes) and
  `artifacts/v5_2/report.json` (FINAL classification
  numbers). Both read-only.
* **RAW reconstruction (Aufgabe 4)** — a fixed
  ``RAW_CONCLUSIONS`` table holds the conclusion text of
  every base chain *before* the v5.2 edit; the four
  prefix variants share the same RAW conclusion. The full
  RAW corpus is recovered by substituting the
  ``Therefore ...`` segment in each FINAL chain.
  ``raw_recovery_rate = 1.000``.
* **Per-chain audit (Aufgabe 5)** — for each pair we
  compute token edit distance, new-probe alignments
  introduced by the rewrite, old-probe avoidances
  removed by the rewrite, Jaccard similarity, and
  whether the ground-truth label changed.
  ``label_preservation_rate = 1.000``.
* **Bias metrics (Aufgabe 6)** — six closed metrics.
* **Counterfactual replay (Aufgabe 7)** — re-run the v5.2
  classifier and probe-transfer audit on the RAW corpus
  with the canonical v5.0 taxonomy and the six safe v5.0
  probes, unchanged.
* **Rewrite influence (Aufgabe 8)** — difference between
  FINAL and RAW for coverage, probe-transfer rate, and
  false-activation count.
* **Negative controls (Aufgabe 9)** — 100 NCs across
  five closed kinds; ``nc_accuracy = 1.000``.

## What the audit found

The cascade classifier is robust to the rewrites:
``coverage_gain_from_rewrites = 0.007``. v5.2 would still
have classified ~93% of new chains into a canonical
``MT_*`` class even without any edit.

The probes are not. ``probe_gain_from_rewrites = 0.49``
and ``false_activation_reduction = 192``. On the RAW
corpus the six v5.0 safe probes (a) only hit 43% of
chains classified into safe-probe classes, and (b)
fire on **192 VALID chains** that they would never
have fired on after the rewrite. The v5.2
``TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES`` verdict was carried entirely by
the engineered probe alignments — the rewrites both
boosted hits on INVALID chains and hid false positives
on VALID chains.

Concretely:

* **53.3% of v5.2 VALID chains** had a safe-probe
  activation in their RAW form that the rewrite removed.
* **97.8% of v5.2 INVALID chains** had no safe-probe
  activation in their RAW form; the rewrite added the
  strict vocabulary (``will`` / ``cannot`` / multi-word
  ``every X``) that triggers MT-P01 / MT-P03.

## Recommendation

**`CORPUS_FIT_TO_TAXONOMY`** — the recommendation gate
triggers on three independent grounds:

* ``probe_gain_from_rewrites = 0.4894 > 0.20``,
* ``false_activation_reduction = 192 > 0`` (probes were
  hidden by the rewrites), and
* five of six bias-metric thresholds fail.

This is a self-audit verdict against v5.2. v5.0's
``METHODOLOGY_TRANSFER_CONFIRMED`` and v5.1's
``TAXONOMY_STABLE`` are not affected: they relied only on
the corpus's structure, not on probe-fire rates.

## Reflection

*When does evaluation data stop testing a taxonomy and
start helping it?*

Five numbers from v5.3:

* **rewrite_fraction = 0.5556** — over half of v5.2's
  chains were edited after first authoring.
* **coverage_gain_from_rewrites = 0.0074** — the
  classifier itself did not need help: it would have
  reached 93% taxonomy coverage on the unedited corpus.
* **probe_gain_from_rewrites = 0.4894** — the *probes*
  needed the edits to hit; the v5.2 hit rate of 0.92
  collapses to 0.43 on the RAW corpus.
* **semantic_shift_mean = 0.3339** — the rewrites were
  not cosmetic; on average a third of the conclusion's
  content tokens changed.
* **recommendation = `CORPUS_FIT_TO_TAXONOMY`**.

Evaluation data stops testing a taxonomy at the point
where the data is rewritten to clear thresholds the data
would otherwise fail. v5.2 crossed that line: the
taxonomy's classifier survives unedited data, but the
*probe-transfer claim* in v5.2 was a measurement of the
rewrites, not of the probes.
