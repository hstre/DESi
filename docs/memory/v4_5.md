* operates *only* on already-extracted `Premise` /

## Reflection

The directive asks: *"When does structural symmetry become
invalid causal support?"*

Using only the v4.4 rescue data, the v4.5 reduction, the
contamination measurement, and the false-cycle rate:

* **5/24** v4.4 residue cases share the same structural
  signature: conclusion content tokens overlap with at least
  two distinct premises, totalling at least three tokens.
  The conclusion **bridges** across premises that did not
  themselves bridge — structural symmetry without causal
  warrant.
* **0/385** protected VALID chains share that signature.
  The pattern is sharp enough to be invariant under benchmark
  prose.
* **1/25** synthetic non-cycle structurally crosses the
  threshold while remaining a legitimate causal chain
  (the "afternoon shift / rising output" edge case).
  The directive's 5% tolerance is the discipline that
  separates symmetric overlap from full-bidirectional cycle.
* **25/25** synthetic cycles fall inside the threshold —
  the pattern is recognisable from the link cardinality
  alone, with no lexical heuristic.

Structural symmetry becomes invalid causal support precisely
when the conclusion *closes a loop* across two or more
premises that did not themselves carry the warrant. The
guard's discipline is to insist on a numerical, lexicon-free
witness of that loop — overlap_premises ≥ 2 *and*
overlap_total ≥ 3 — and to keep its false-cycle rate at the
boundary of what the protected pool tolerates.
