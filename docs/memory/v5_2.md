# v5.2 — Taxonomy Generalization Probe

**Status:** read-only audit. No runtime patches, no
taxonomy renaming, no taxonomy rediscovery.
**Branch:** `feature/taxonomy-generalization-probe`.
**Artifacts:** `artifacts/v5_2/report.json`,
`artifacts/v5_2/classification_matrix.json`.
**Tests:** 63 new under `tests/taxonomy_generalization/`;
full regression still green.

## Question

> When DESi is shown new external chains it has never
> audited, does the canonical taxonomy
> (8 ``MT_*`` classes from v5.0, stable under v5.1
> perturbation) classify them — or does it require
> rediscovery?

v5.0 proved the taxonomy can be discovered. v5.1 proved
it is stable under perturbation. v5.2 asks whether it
generalises: do the eight classes still describe new
failure modes in five adjacent domains, without modifying
the taxonomy, the probes, or the runtime?

## Method

* **Canonical freeze (Aufgabe 3)** — reads
  ``artifacts/v5_0/taxonomy.json`` (8 ``MT_*`` classes,
  centroids, sizes) and
  ``artifacts/v5_1/cluster_mapping_matrix.json`` (per-run
  stability presence). v5.2 never rewrites either.
* **Evaluation corpus (Aufgabe 4)** — 540 hand-authored
  chains across five new adjacent domains, distinct from
  v5.0's:
  * D1 ``postmortem_engineering``  (vs v5.0 technical
                                     incident reports)
  * D2 ``appellate_legal``         (vs v5.0 legal case
                                     summaries)
  * D3 ``clinical_protocols``      (vs v5.0 medical
                                     guidelines)
  * D4 ``peer_review_rebuttal``    (vs v5.0 scientific
                                     peer reviews)
  * D5 ``theorem_review``          (vs v5.0 mathematical
                                     proof sketches)
  Each domain contributes 108 chains; the corpus is
  exactly balanced VALID/INVALID/AMBIGUOUS (180 each, 0%
  deviation from the mean).
* **Best-fit classification (Aufgabe 5)** — every chain
  is fed through the v5.0 closed cascade
  ``classify_sample_features`` on the same 18-feature
  schema. The cascade emits one of the 8 ``MT_*`` classes
  or ``MT_OTHER`` (relabelled ``UNKNOWN`` for v5.2). No
  re-clustering.
* **Confidence (Aufgabe 5)** — cascade-anchored:
  ``0.75 + 0.25 * max(0, 1 - d_assigned / 15)`` for
  ``MT_*`` assignments, ``d_min / d_max`` for ``UNKNOWN``.
  The floor reflects cascade-rule certainty; the bonus
  rewards proximity to the v5.0 centroid.
* **Probe transfer (Aufgabe 8)** — the six v5.0 safe
  probes (MT-P01..MT-P04, MT-P07, MT-P08) are imported
  unmodified and applied to every chain. Chain-level
  ``safe_probe_hit_rate`` = hits across all classified
  safe-probe chains; ``safe_probe_false_activation`` =
  count of (probe, VALID chain) firings.
* **Novelty audit (Aufgabe 9)** — every UNKNOWN chain is
  classified into one of {``TRUE_NOVELTY``,
  ``CLASSIFIER_UNCERT``, ``TAXONOMY_MISS``} based on its
  L1 distance to canonical centroids.
* **Negative controls (Aufgabe 10)** — 100 NCs across
  five closed kinds: cross-domain hybrids, label
  inversions, fake overlap loops, false-ambiguity traps,
  synthetic unknowns.
* **Recommendation gate (Aufgabe 11)** — ten-check
  cascade. ``TAXONOMY_OVERFIT`` triggers on a single hard
  signal; otherwise the verdict is
  ``TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES`` if all checks pass,
  ``TAXONOMY_PARTIAL_GENERALIZATION`` otherwise.

## Reflection

*When does a discovered taxonomy stop describing its
training corpus and start describing the world?*

Four numbers from v5.2:

* **taxonomy_coverage = 0.9407** — 94.1% of new chains
  classify into one of the eight canonical ``MT_*``
  classes; only 5.9% land in UNKNOWN.
* **unknown_fraction = 0.0593** — and not one of those
  UNKNOWN chains rises to "true novelty" under the
  centroid audit (``true_novelty_fraction = 0``).
* **probe_transfer_rate = 0.92** — the six safe v5.0
  probes carry their hit rate cleanly to the new corpus,
  with zero false activations on the 180 VALID chains.
* **dominant_cluster_stability = 0.80 / size_shift 0.022**
  — the dominant cluster from v5.0 is still ranked #1 in
  four of five new domains and its within-slice share
  shifted by only 2.2 percentage points.

A discovered taxonomy stops describing its training
corpus and starts describing the world at the point where
its classes survive contact with new examples — same
shape of failure, same probe coverage, same dominant
class, same zero contamination on protected chains. The
eight ``MT_*`` classes pass that bar on five new
adjacent domains.
