## Question

> When does a chain look coherent but lack an actual warrant?

v4.3 and v4.5 retired everything that admitted a
contamination-zero surface or structural intervention. The
remaining 19 false-supports survive every existing guard.
v4.6 asks what *kind* of warrant they are missing, and
whether any of five plausible warrant probes could rescue
them without contaminating the protected pool.

## Method

* **Case selection:** the 19 chains in the v4.0 corpus that
  still audit as `LOGICALLY_SUPPORTED + CAUSAL_CHAIN` under
  the v4.5-patched auditor.
* **Replay:** extended replay records the per-premise and
  per-conclusion content tokens, the cross-premise overlap,
  the support path, and the originating v4.1 strategies.
* **Classification:** a deterministic priority cascade
  assigns each case *exactly one* `WarrantFailure` value
  (9 closed values).
* **Counterfactual probes (W1–W5):** five simulations of
  alternative warrant requirements. Each is a predicate
  evaluated on every residue case (and on every protected
  VALID chain).
* **Negative controls:** 60 hand-authored warrant fixtures —
  20 missing-bridge, 20 scope-extension, 20
  generalization-leap.

## Recommendation

**`WARRANT_FAILURE_LOCALIZED`** — the 19 residual
false-supports are not diffuse. They concentrate into three
named families: contradiction (9), correlation-to-causation
(5), and sample-to-universal (5). One safe probe (W3) covers
10/19 with zero contamination; the remaining 9 contradictions
require a content-level audit outside the v4.6 scope.

## Reflection

The directive asks: *"When does a conclusion stop following
from premises — and start merely sounding compatible?"*

Using only counts, rescue rates, contamination, and
agreement:

* **3 named clusters** absorb 19/19 cases; the residue is not
  diffuse (entropy = 1.524 bits, three families).
* **0 UNKNOWN** — the closed warrant taxonomy fully covers
  the post-marker, post-structural residue.
* **1 safe probe rescues 10/19** (52.6%). W3 catches every
  modality-shift case (past observational premise + modal /
  future conclusion) with zero contamination — a structural
  asymmetry the audit can lawfully inspect without semantic
  understanding.
* **0/19 cases are blocked by all safe probes** — the safe
  probes do not overlap; W2 finds no v4.6 case, W3 finds
  the modality cluster.
* **9/19 cases admit no safe probe** — they are direct
  premise/conclusion contradictions with no surface
  distinction from a coherent inference. The audit would
  need a content-level NLI step to mark them invalid.

A conclusion stops following from premises and starts merely
sounding compatible precisely when the warrant connecting
them is asymmetric in tense or modality (W3 catches this),
or when the warrant would require a *bridge rule* that
contradicts the premise itself (W1 catches this but at
catastrophic contamination cost). The 9 MISSING_BRIDGE_RULE
cases are the boundary the v4.6 probe bank cannot lawfully
cross.
