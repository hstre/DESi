# v5.4 — Raw-Corpus Taxonomy Generalization Probe

**Status:** read-only re-evaluation. No runtime patches,
no taxonomy renaming, no probe modifications, no corpus
rewrites.
**Branch:** `feature/raw-corpus-generalization-probe`.
**Artifacts:** `artifacts/v5_4/report.json`,
`artifacts/v5_4/split_eval.json`. v5.3 also gained an
additive `artifacts/v5_3/raw_corpus.json` (frozen RAW
chains, no existing v5.3 artifact altered).
**Tests:** 46 new under `tests/raw_generalization/`; full
regression still green.

## Question

> What survives when all human probe-alignment is
> removed?

v5.2 reported `TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES` on a corpus
v5.3 then proved was rewritten to fit the probes (rewrite
fraction 0.56, probe gain from rewrites 0.49, 192 hidden
false activations). v5.4 re-runs the v5.2 evaluation on
the v5.3 RAW corpus, with the taxonomy and probe
channels evaluated independently.

## Method

* **Frozen inputs (Aufgabe 3)** —
  `artifacts/v5_0/taxonomy.json`,
  `artifacts/v5_2/report.json`,
  `artifacts/v5_3/raw_corpus.json` (additive companion
  to v5.3's existing artifacts). 8 ``MT_*`` classes and
  6 safe probes, both read-only.
* **Evaluation corpus (Aufgabe 4)** — the 540 RAW chains
  loaded directly from the v5.3 artifact. Balance:
  VALID 180 / INVALID 180 / AMBIGUOUS 180. No
  substitutions, no paraphrases, no exclusions.
* **Split evaluation (Aufgabe 5)** — every claim
  declares its channel:
  * ``taxonomy_only`` channel: ``classify_all`` cascade
    from v5.2 applied unmodified.
  * ``probe_only`` channel: six v5.0 safe probes applied
    unmodified via the v5.2 ``audit_probe_transfer``
    helper.
  The two channels are never aggregated into a single
  metric.
* **Negative controls (Aufgabe 9)** — 100 NCs across
  five closed kinds (raw-valid probe traps,
  raw-invalid paraphrases, ambiguity decoys, overlap
  illusions, domain hybrids). NC accuracy gates both
  channel assignments simultaneously.

## Recommendation

**`TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES_PROBES_FAIL`** — the gate
triggers cleanly:

* taxonomy thresholds: 6/6 ✓
* probe thresholds:    1/3 ✓
* nc_accuracy:         ≥ 0.95 ✓

The cascade classifier survives the corpus rewrites that
the probes did not. v5.0's
``METHODOLOGY_TRANSFER_CONFIRMED`` and v5.1's
``TAXONOMY_STABLE`` are unaffected. v5.2's combined
``TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES`` verdict is corrected to a
split one: the taxonomy claim was real; the
probe-transfer claim was carried by the rewrites
v5.3 already documented.

## Reflection

*When the scaffolding is removed, what part of the
method still stands?*

Five numbers from v5.4:

* **taxonomy_coverage = 0.9333** — 93.3% of RAW chains
  still classify into a canonical ``MT_*`` class.
* **taxonomy_unknown_fraction = 0.0667** — and only
  6.7% land in UNKNOWN, well under the 10% ceiling.
* **probe_hit_rate = 0.4306** — but the six safe probes
  only hit 43% of chains classified into their classes
  once the strict vocabulary is removed from the
  conclusions.
* **probe_false_activation = 192** — and on the
  unedited corpus the same probes fire on 192 VALID
  chains the rewrites had hidden.
* **recommendation = `TAXONOMY_GENERALIZES_PROBES_FAIL`**.

The taxonomy stands. The probes do not. The methodology
that survives is closed-cascade classification on the
v5.0 feature schema; the safe-probe predicates, as
written, are too narrow to transfer without corpus
shaping.
