## Question

> When is a marker extension legitimate, and when is it just
> overfitting?

v4.2 localised 143 v4.0 false-supports into a closed
ten-value taxonomy. Three clusters
(HIDDEN_NEGATION, QUANTIFIER_DRIFT, AUTHORITY_CONTAMINATION)
together cover 119/143 = 83.2% of the residue, and *every*
candidate token in those clusters came back from the v4.2
counterfactual probe with `contamination = 0` against the
existing protected benchmarks. v4.3 takes the most disciplined
possible step: extend the v3.16 marker tuples with *only* the
tokens v4.2 evidence justifies.

1. Observed in a v4.2 target-cluster case,

LedgerEvent, no benchmark relabeling, no regex edits.

### Tokens *not* added (overfitting risk)

* CYCLE_DISGUISE's v4.2 tokens are content tokens
  (`clause`, `law`, `matter`) — they are not connective
  patterns and so violate the closed marker-family discipline.
  CYCLE_DISGUISE is intentionally left as the open v4.3
  residue.
* `' sole '` and `' established '` (raw): each appears in
  v4.0-VALID chains (`will named the eldest child as sole
  beneficiary`, `infection is established`) and would have
  caused false negatives. Replaced with the safer composite
  forms `' sole cause '` and `' conclusively established '`.

## Reflection

The directive asks: *"When is a marker extension legitimate,
and when is it just overfitting?"*

By the v4.3 evidence:

* **Legitimate:** the 29 tokens that survived four filters
  — observed in v4.2 cluster, in the closed marker family,
  counterfactual_reduction ≥ 5, contamination 0 across the
  protected pool. Together they retire 119/143 = 83.2% of
  the residue.
* **Overfitting:** the four tokens v4.2 surfaced that
  *failed* the contamination filter (`sole`, `established`,
  `clause`, `law`). Adding them would have caused
  false-negative regressions on valid benchmark chains.
  Their exclusion is the discipline that distinguishes a
  marker extension from a guess.
* **Genuinely open:** the 24 residual cases that v4.2
  classified as CYCLE_DISGUISE / FRAME_SWITCH /
  SEMANTIC_NON_SEQUITUR. None of them carry a closed-family
  surface marker; their invalidity is semantic. They are the
  open problem a future audit-level upgrade (outside the
  v4.3 scope) would have to address.

The boundary between legitimate extension and overfitting is
not stylistic. It is the conjunction of four numerical
filters. v4.3's discipline is to refuse every token that
fails any one of them.
