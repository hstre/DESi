> property — and become a documented historical fact?

machine-checkable claims and a 15-section paper. v4.11

proved that nine of the v4 pins are

reproducibility claim now carries its v4.11 class

## Reflection

The directive asks: *"When does a reproducible result stop
being a runtime property — and become a documented
historical fact?"*

Using only the v4.12 counts:

* **127** total claims; zero deleted; zero claim_ids
  reassigned. Every prior assertion is preserved.
* **22** of those claims received the v4.11 reproducibility
  metadata. Distribution: 12 `FROZEN_ARTIFACT_REPLAYABLE`,
  9 `HISTORICAL_RUNTIME_DRIFT`, 1 `LIVE_REPLAY_STABLE`.
* **0** contradictions — the paper no longer carries any
  phrase implying live-replay stability for versions whose
  matrix entry says otherwise.
* **22** annotated claims carry an `environment_scope`
  field (one of `frozen-only`, `frozen+drift`,
  `frozen+live-stable`).

A reproducible result stops being a runtime property and
becomes a documented historical fact at the point where
its claim references a **frozen artifact file** rather
than a **live re-execution**. v4.12's discipline is to
make that boundary explicit per claim, annotate twenty-two
of them with the appropriate class, and verify that no
paper text contradicts the matrix.
